Articles

A collection of selected articles by Abdullah.

In the Conceptual Framework of al-Kindi al-’Omani

This article delves into the philosophical contributions of Abu Bakr Ahmed bin Abdullah al-Kindi, an Omani scholar from Nizwa. It highlights his emphasis on precise definitions and logical reasoning, particularly in his discussions on the indivisibility of the “essence” or “substance” (جوهر). Al-Kindi advocates for returning to the true definitions of concepts to avoid contradictions and misunderstandings in philosophical discourse.

Read The Arabic Article

Creatures of Intellectual thinker: Proposing the Model

Thoughts behave like living entities—interacting, evolving, and shaping our perception. This article explores how ideas cluster into belief systems, resist change, or collapse under contradiction. It reveals how thought structures compete, transform, and influence knowledge over time.

Read The Arabic Article

The Sun on the Breakfast Table

Reflecting on a hot June day in Muscat, the author contemplates the omnipresence of the sun and its profound impact on daily life. The article discusses the sun’s composition, its relationship with Earth, and its influence on human activities and natural phenomena, aiming to convey scientific concepts in an accessible manner.

Read The Arabic Article

The Human as a Linking Animal

This article examines the concept of humans as “linking animals,” emphasizing the unique human ability to create connections between disparate ideas, objects, and individuals. It discusses how this capacity for linkage underpins cultural development, communication, and technological advancement.

Read The Arabic Article

The Phenomenon Across Conceptual Systems

The article analyzes how different conceptual frameworks interpret and understand various phenomena. It argues that the same event or idea can be perceived differently depending on the underlying conceptual system, highlighting the importance of perspective in knowledge acquisition.

Read The Arabic Article

The Loss of Textual Pleasure

This piece laments the diminishing joy of engaging with texts in the modern age. It explores factors contributing to this decline, such as the rise of digital media, shortened attention spans, and the shift towards superficial reading habits, advocating for a return to deep and meaningful engagement with written works.

Read The Arabic Article

I Am the Supreme Intellectual

In this introspective article, the author reflects on the role of the intellectual in society. It discusses the responsibilities, challenges, and potential hubris associated with intellectualism, emphasizing the need for humility, continuous learning, and engagement with diverse perspectives.
